About This Site About the Commonwealth Legislative Drafting Manual This site provides a navigable, searchable web display of the Commonwealth Legislative Drafting Manual. The Manual originated in 1975 in response to a recognized shortage of experienced legislative drafters within Commonwealth member countries, and was first published in 1976.
1 Background and History Over the years, the manual has undergone significant updates to reflect contemporary drafting styles and practices across the Commonwealth, including an increased emphasis on plain language and accessibility in legislation. The Hon. Justice V.C.R.A.C. Crabbe was tasked with revising the manual in 2013, which was endorsed by Commonwealth Law Ministers in 2014. It was further updated in 2016-2017 by Mr. Roger Rose and reviewed by Offices of Attorneys General across the Commonwealth. The Commonwealth Legislative Drafting Manual continues to serve as a vital tool for legislative drafters, contributing to the clarity, comprehensiveness, and consistency of legislation within the Commonwealth.
2 Experimental Transformation This is an experimental transformation of the official Commonwealth Legislative Drafting Manual from PDF format to HTML for web display. This project aims to make the Manual more accessible by converting it into a web-friendly format with enhanced navigation. The transformation from PDF to XHTML was accomplished using advanced Large Language Models (LLMs), primarily Google Gemini 3.1 Pro. The pipeline to convert and structure the raw PDFs into organized HTML content, as well as the robust display UI, is available open-source. For the generic conversion engine used to build this site, please visit the pdf-convert-llm repository.
